Diffusion Models

Chapter 7 · Generative AI — Visualize the forward and reverse diffusion process

Diffusion models generate data by learning to reverse a gradual noising process. In the forward process, structured data is slowly destroyed by adding Gaussian noise over many timesteps. The reverse process learns to undo this, recovering clean data from pure noise. Watch how particles transform between structure and randomness.

Forward Process

Starting from clean data x₀, we gradually add Gaussian noise over T timesteps. At each step, a small amount of noise controlled by βt is mixed in. The closed-form formula xₜ = √ᾱₜ · x₀ + √(1-ᾱₜ) · ε lets us jump to any timestep directly.

Reverse Process

A neural network learns to predict the noise ε added at each step, then subtracts it to recover the signal. Moving from t=T (pure noise) back to t=0 (clean data) generates new samples. Here we show the exact reverse using stored noise, no network needed.
