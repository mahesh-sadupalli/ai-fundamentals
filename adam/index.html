What is Adam?

Adam combines Momentum (first moment) with RMSProp (second moment) into a single adaptive optimizer. It maintains per-parameter learning rates that scale inversely with gradient variance.

w -= α · m̂/(√v̂ + ε)

First Moment (Mean)

The exponential moving average of gradients acts like momentum, smoothing out noise and accelerating along consistent directions.

m = β₁·m + (1-β₁)·g

Second Moment (Variance)

The EMA of squared gradients tracks per-parameter gradient magnitude. Large gradients get dampened, small ones get boosted — equalizing progress.

v = β₂·v + (1-β₂)·g²

Bias Correction

Since m and v are initialized at zero, early estimates are biased toward zero. Dividing by (1-βᵗ) corrects this, giving accurate estimates from step 1.

m̂ = m/(1-β₁¹)   v̂ = v/(1-β₂¹)
