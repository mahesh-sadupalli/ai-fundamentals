What is SGD?

In batch gradient descent, we compute the exact gradient over all training data. Stochastic gradient descent uses a random subset (mini-batch), producing a noisy but unbiased gradient estimate. Update: w -= lr * (grad + noise).

Why Noise Helps

SGD's noise helps escape sharp local minima and saddle points that trap batch GD. The stochasticity acts as implicit regularization, guiding the optimizer toward flat minima that generalize better to unseen data.

Mini-batch Trade-off

Larger batches give more accurate gradients but cost more compute. Noise scales as 1/√batch_size. Batch size 32–256 is a sweet spot: enough noise to regularize, enough signal to converge efficiently.

Learning Rate & Noise

Higher learning rates amplify gradient noise, causing wilder oscillations. In practice, use learning rate warmup and decay schedules to balance fast early progress with stable convergence. lr ∝ √batch_size is a common scaling rule.
